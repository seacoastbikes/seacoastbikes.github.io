ADVOCACY (DRAFT)

SABR was formed in 1992 as an advocacy organization to promote safe and effective bicycling networks in the Seacoast region of New Hampshire and Southern Maine.

As a nall-volunteer group, SABR has poured our time and passion into advocating for local bicycle infrastructure and helping to incubate new biking initiatives.

  • SABR worked with Tenants Association at Pease to create the Pease Multi-Use Path 2015
  • We advocated for the creation of the Rockingham Bicycle/Pedestrian bridge off the Portsmouth Traffic Circle. 1999

Biking Program Incubator

  • Bike to Work Day. SABR laid the foundation for statewide Bike/Walk Days by using a grant from the New Hampshire Charitable Foundation to organize bike groups throughout the state. Since 2003.
  • Seacoast Velokids. SABR was the parent organization of this freeprogram begun in 2013 to teach teaches elementary and middle school kids how to ride mountain bikes in the woods.
  • Bike Racing - SABR was an incubator for the Portsmouth Criterium and Exeter Criterium.
  • **Women Only -**SheRIDES Seacoast became an arm of SABR in 2016. This women-only group empowers and encourages women to bicycle through educational clinics, fun rides, and other activities.
  • Businesses - SABR spearheaded local implementation of the national Bicycle Benefits program. This program provides discounts at member businesses to cyclists who purchase a $5 sticker or card and ride to the businesses. This program shows the value of cyclists to area businesses.
